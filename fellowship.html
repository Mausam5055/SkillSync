The Fellowship Experience.

The SkillSync Fellowship is a paid, part-time program that prepares educators to launch or lead a school built to last.
01

Formation and Learning

Fellows enter a season of formation: learning our model, engaging with our culture, assessing mutual fit with our philosophy, and practicing the habits of leadership that shape our communities.
02

Training and Support

Fellows dedicate 5–10 hours each week, split between live programming in the evenings, asynchronous online coursework, and fieldwork in their communities alongside campus teams.
03

Evaluation and Readiness

Every assignment and evaluation measures readiness to lead, ensuring that those who complete the Fellowship are proven, prepared, and ready to make an impact.

Frequently Asked Questions.

Is the Fellowship paid?

Yes. Fellows receive a monthly stipend while completing the part-time program, along with full support from the Fellowship team.

How long does the program last?

Launch Track Fellows complete an eight-month program. Join Track Fellows train four months before stepping into leadership roles.

How much time does it take?

Fellows dedicate 5–10 hours each week to live programming, online coursework, and fieldwork alongside SkillSync’s campus teams.

What happens after completion?

Graduates transition into full-time SkillSync Leader roles—opening new campuses or joining existing ones to lead classrooms and communities.

Who should apply?

We’re looking for exceptional educators ready to grow into leadership—people who believe in rigor, care, and taking kids seriously.
